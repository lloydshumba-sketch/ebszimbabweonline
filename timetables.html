Academic Rhythm

The June 2026 session is built around consistency, practice and feedback.

The timetable is supported by a learning cycle that helps students move from technical coverage to exam-ready performance.

Academic Block Purpose Student Action Delivery Mode
Live Tuition Block Online Technical explanation, examiner focus, scenario analysis and direct teaching. Attend live class, ask questions and update study notes. Zoom Workplace / live online class
Recorded Review Block Replay Replay difficult topics and consolidate areas covered during live sessions. Rewatch recordings, summarise weak areas and prepare questions. Recorded lecture access
Question Practice Block Practice Convert knowledge into exam-standard written answers. Attempt selected questions under timed or structured conditions. Independent practice and tutor guidance
Marked Feedback Block Feedback Identify lost marks, weak application and poor answer structure. Submit work, review comments and correct answer technique. Marked assignments and feedback
Revision Control Block Revision Refine exam readiness, timing, technical recall and professional presentation. Review weak areas and prepare for final practice cycles. Guided revision and self-study
Phase 01

Technical Foundation

Students build the knowledge base through live explanations, structured notes and topic-by-topic academic guidance.

Phase 02

Scenario Application

Students move from learning the content to applying it in scenarios, calculations, audit issues and professional discussion.

Phase 03

Revision and Exam Control

Students refine timing, answer structure, topic prioritisation and final examination technique.
